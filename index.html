About Me

My name is Sebastiano Busato, and I'm currently a Postdoctoral Research Scholar at the EnBiSys Lab @ North Carolina State University

My research focuses on the intersection between big data and the life sciences, particularly agriculture and livestock. I received a B.S. in Agronomy from Zamorano University in Honduras in 2016, and a Ph.D. in Animal Sciences (with minors in Molecular & Cell Biology and Biological Data Sciences) from Oregon State University in 2021 (go Beavs!).

My doctoral work revolved around the interaction between nutrient and genes in ruminants, particularly the nutrigenomics effect of fatty acids in periparturient dairy cows. Currently, I study the structure and regulation of gene networks (such as the gap gene network in Drosophila melanogaster), and the application of Machine Learning methodologies in genomics, metagenomics and agricultural datasets.

I have been fortunate to develop my work both in the field and at the lab bench, and to develop a healthy programming and data analysis expertise.

For more info on my work, check out my publications, my personal and professional projects, and my CV.

Projects

This is a series of personal projects I've done over the years:

  • RSViz (2023): a dashboard written in R-Shiny to report hospitalization rates (change over week, weekly rates and cumulative rates) due to Respiratory Syncitial Virus (RSV) in the 12 US States that participate in the RSV-Net project. A second tab also reports positivity rate trends over multiple years (until 2020).
    This leverages the RSV-NET database from the CDC API at data.cdc.gov. I used the RSocrata package developed by the city of Chicago, and created a simple query that returns average rates for all Sex, Race, and Age category.
  • DSPG_Dash (2020): another R-shiny dashboard that looks at air and water temperatures at different locations along the Deschutes River in Central Oregon.
    I developed this viz for a Data Science for the Public Good project, and it uses the rnoaa package to scrape NOAA data. Unfortunately, the package has since been deprecated, and though NOAA promised they would replace it, they haven't yet, so the Dashboard is kinda broken. Fixing this is on my to-do list, though I'm hoping they'll release a new package in the interim hehe
